# 📚 Community studies

This is where the community publishes its research on $DOG. Anyone can add one — **@Cryptolution and any DOG who brings receipts.** Each study is a single Markdown file. Published studies are linked from the site (**dogarmy.space**).

## How to add a study (two ways)

**A) The easy way — no coding** 🟢
Open a **[New issue → "📚 Submit a study"](../../issues/new/choose)** and paste your study. A maintainer turns it into a file here and credits you.

**B) The git way — you own and update it yourself** 🔧
1. Copy [`_TEMPLATE.md`](_TEMPLATE.md).
2. Name your file `YYYY-MM-DD-short-slug.md` (e.g. `2026-06-28-gate-proof-of-reserves.md`).
3. Fill it in (see the rules below).
4. Open a pull request. Once merged, **you can update your own file anytime** with new PRs.

> Want to push directly without PRs? Ask the maintainer ([@dogdamassa](https://github.com/dogdamassa)) to add you as a collaborator — recommended for regular contributors like @Cryptolution.

## The rules (what makes a study count)

Every study must:
- **Lead with one plain sentence** — what you found.
- **Show a public source** — a URL, API call, on-chain address, or command.
- **Be reproducible** — anyone can re-run your steps and get your numbers.
- **Carry a confidence grade:** ✅ confirmed · 🟡 strong signal / watching · ⚖️ not proven (limit).
- **State caveats** — the limits of the interpretation.
- **Credit you** — your name/handle in the header.

And must **never**: name a person/company as a criminal without independent proof, predict price, or call buys/sells. We show behavior, not intent. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md) and the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
